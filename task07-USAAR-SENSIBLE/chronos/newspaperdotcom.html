8 THE COLONIES AND INDIA DEC. 11,1897 The Rev. F. J. Tackley, M.A., Curate of Macclesfield, has been appointed Precentor of Cape Town Cathedral. The report that Mr. F. M. Hodgson, C.M.G., the Colonial Secretary of the Gold Coast, had been appointed Administrator of the Gambia, in place of Mr. R. B. Llewelyn, C.M.G., was somewhat premature, but was founded upon a strong rumour in official circles. Mr.- Llewelyn will for the present continue to carry on his appointment. In the meantime, Mr. Hodgson is acting as Governor of the Gold Coast during the absence on leave of Sir William Maxwell—an office he has satisfactorily filled on several occasions, with oredit to himself and satisfaction to those serving under him. *** Mr. George Stallard, Chief Justice of Sierra Leono, has arrived in England on leave of absence. *** Mr. William Clark, Polioe Magistrate of Sierra Leone, is coming to England^on leave of absence, and was due to leave the Colony on the arrival at Freetown of Mr. de Groot, the Solicitor-General. Mr. Clark has lately been acting as Attorney-General. *** Mr. William Clark will not return to Sierra Leone on the expiration of his leave, as he has been selected to succeed Sir Neville Geary, Bart., as Attorney-General of the Gold Coast. Mr. Clark is to be congratulated on his promotion, which financially is an important one, as the salary of his new office commences at 800£. and rises to 1,000?. a year. British Guiana is becoming well represented on the Gold Coast, both Mr. William Nicoll and Mr. William Clark now occupying important legal appointments there. Mr. Anatole Sauzier has been appointed British Consul for Madagascar, to reside at Tamatave. *** The 2nd Battalion of the Royal Berkshire Regiment, from Aldershot, has been selected as an addition to the reliefs 1897-98 to proceed to South Africa to replace the 1st Battalion of the Middlesex Regiment, who will go from South Africa to India. *** Colonel W. M. Playfair, A.P.D., has been appointed Chief Paymaster in South Afrioa, vice Colonel G. H. Anson. Lieutenant Robert J. F. Taylor, Derbyshire Regiment, is about to be seoonded from November 27 for service under the Colonial Office in connection with Lagos. *** Lieutenant Frank Bevan, Northumberland Fusiliers, will shortly be seconded from November 27 for service in Lagos. *** Lieutenant Frank F. Fenton, 11th Hussars, is about to be seconded from November 20 for service in the Niger Coast Protectorate. * ^ * Government orders for hundreds of saddles, sets of harness, &c, have just been placed with Birmingham firms, and inquiries have been made in Bristol, Warwick, and other large towns for a large supply of mule carts, 250 of which are required for immediate shipment to the Uganda Protectorate, which is also the destination of the saddlery above referred to. *** The Queen has been graciously pleased to appoint Henry Charles Dundas, Esq., to be Her Majesty's Vice-Consul in German East Africa, to reside at Dar-es-Salaam. *** It has been decided that the 7th Hussars shall remain for another year in South Africa. Xhe regiment will, in all probability, be brought to England next winter. *** Captain Cromer Ashburnham, 2nd Battalion King's Royal Rifles, has been selected for employment in Uganda.
